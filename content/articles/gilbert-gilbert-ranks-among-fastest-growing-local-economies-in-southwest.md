The update adds to a string of recent economic developments across Gilbert, part of a broader trend reshaping local balance sheets this year.

"This is something our community has been asking for, and we're glad to finally see it come together," said Jennifer Martin, chamber of commerce director in Gilbert.

Analysts point to Gilbert's expanding workforce and comparatively low cost of doing business relative to coastal markets. The shift is expected to affect roughly 26 million dollars in local economic activity over the next two years, according to figures reviewed by the Copper State Chronicle.

> "We're proud of what this means for Gilbert and the people who live here." — Jennifer Martin

Local officials say they will continue tracking the impact on jobs and tax revenue in the months ahead.
